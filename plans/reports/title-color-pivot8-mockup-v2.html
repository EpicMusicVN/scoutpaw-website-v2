Pivot #8 v2 — Brand-fitting title color exploration (open beyond blue/yellow)

5 new directions + 1 hybrid carryover. Card-internal titles (Say hi to Max, Cozy Season Bundle) stay ink-blue in every variant per locked two-tier rule.

What's different from mockup v1

v1 only explored the blue/yellow combinations you originally chose. v2 honors your "do not limit to current implementation" brief and brings in 3 new palette directions: cinnamon, plum, and coral. Each is paired with a section surface that yields AA-safe contrast — no physics bans this time.

The 6 directions

B1 solid gold on cyan cyan (unchanged) solid gold ink-blue ~4.5:1 Minimal change. Drops gradient, keeps warm. Safest option.
B2 honey on dark anchor ink (#2b1d10) brand-primary honey muted ~14:1 Premium "rich dark + honey." Bold, confident. Pivot #6-ish but using --ink (coffee brown), not --ink-blue.
B3 cinnamon on cream cream cinnamon ink-blue ~5.2:1 Warm earth-cozy. Reads like a children's book cover. Departs from current palette but coheres.
B4 plum on warm-tan warm-tan plum ink-blue ~9:1 Premium playful. Plum signals craft + maturity; warm-tan keeps it cozy. Strongest "elevated" feel.
B5 coral on ink-blue cyan (unchanged) cinnamon coral / ink-blue ~5:1 Hero pops with coral on navy; banners settle into warm cinnamon. High contrast + playful. Two-color system, more dynamic.
B6 gradient (current pivot #7) ink-blue strip brand-primary brand-primary muted ~10:1 Hybrid carryover from v1 (was A4). Surgical change — only h2 banners flip; hero unchanged. Lowest implementation cost.

How to choose

Scroll each frame. Don't judge against a card or a single section — judge the full-page rhythm. That's the lesson from pivot #6.

  • Prioritize playful + premium? → B4 (plum on warm-tan) or B5 (coral hero + cinnamon banners).
  • Prioritize warm cozy familiar? → B1 (solid gold) or B3 (cinnamon on cream).
  • Prioritize bold + confident? → B2 (honey on dark anchor).
  • Prioritize minimal disruption + surgical implementation? → B6 (hybrid).
