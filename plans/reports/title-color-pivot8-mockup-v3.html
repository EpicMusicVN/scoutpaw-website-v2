Pivot #8 v3 — Bluey-vibe palettes (playful kids brand)

5 directions. Cream-warm body bg replaces cyan (closer to Bluey-on-paper feel). Saturated cobalt + coral + honey instead of muted gold. Card titles still ink-blue.

What changed from v2

You said v2 was "wrong direction entirely" and wanted Bluey-vibe playful kids brand. v3 swaps the body bg from cyan to warm cream (Bluey's signature paper-color), introduces a brighter cobalt (deeper than navy, more saturated than ink-blue), warmer coral, and brings the honey in as a kicker accent (not a title). Card surfaces still light, card titles still ink-blue.

D1 cream-warm cobalt cobalt honey ~10:1 Bluey signature: confident cobalt titles with sunny honey kickers. Most "official kids brand" feel.
D2 cream-warm coral-warm coral-warm cobalt ~5.5:1 Punchy coral on cream. Highest energy. Reads as warm + alive.
D3 cyan (unchanged) honey w/ cobalt outline honey w/ cobalt outline cobalt ~10:1 (outline) Sticker-pop: honey yellow titles with cobalt outline. Yellow stays vibrant; outline provides AA. Strongest "sticker" / "Saturday-morning-cartoon" feel.

Where this lands relative to Bluey

Bluey itself uses cobalt + honey + coral on a warm cream/paper background, with bold display type. D1 / D4 / D5 hit that aesthetic most directly. D2 is hotter (more Bluey-coral-card-game). D3 is the most "sticker pop" — closest to the Saturday-morning cartoon energy if that's the vibe you want.
